Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India & Former President

About Dr. Kalam

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, popularly known as the Missile Man of India, was one of the nation’s most respected scientists and served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, his remarkable journey from humble beginnings to the highest office in the country remains a source of inspiration.

Dr. Kalam was known for his vision, simplicity, and dedication towards igniting young minds. His books like "Wings of Fire" remain inspirational classics. He passed away on 27 July 2015 while delivering a lecture, leaving behind a legacy of knowledge and motivation.

Early Life & Education

Dr. Kalam came from a modest background. His father was a boat owner and imam of a local mosque. Despite financial challenges, Kalam was a brilliant student with a deep curiosity about science and engineering. He graduated in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and then studied Aerospace Engineering at Madras Institute of Technology (MIT).

Vision & Legacy

Dr. Kalam was deeply passionate about transforming India into a developed nation. He launched "Vision 2020" — a plan to make India economically developed by focusing on technology, agriculture, and education. He often interacted with students, encouraging them to dream big and work hard.

Even after his presidency, he continued to travel across India delivering lectures and motivating millions. His humility, wisdom, and infectious optimism continue to inspire generations.

Notable Achievements

  • Key figure in India's missile development programs
  • Chief Scientific Adviser to the Government of India
  • 11th President of India (2002-2007)
  • Recipient of Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award
  • Authored inspirational books like "Wings of Fire"
  • Launched Vision 2020 for India's development
